The White-Bellied Bird

The white-bellied go-away bird gets its name from its distinctive call, which sounds like "go-away." It has grey plumage, a white belly, and a large crest on top of its head. The bird also has white markings on its tail, which become visible when it is in flight.

White-bellied go-away birds inhabit the arid savannahs and woodlands of Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda, Sudan, South Sudan, Somalia, and Ethiopia.

The IUCN lists this bird as "Least Concern," meaning there are no major threats to its survival at present.
